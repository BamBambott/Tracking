How TrackNG Works

From the moment your goods leave Lagos to delivery in Ibadan — here's exactly what happens.

THE PROCESS
01

Goods Are Registered

When a shipment is booked, goods get a unique Tracking ID tied to the route, sender, and recipient.

02

Checkpoints Are Logged

As the vehicle moves, agents at each stop confirm the goods. The system records the timestamp and updates status instantly.

03

You Track in Real-Time

Enter your Tracking ID and see exactly which checkpoint your goods have reached. Updates live — no refresh needed.

04

Share With Your Recipient

Copy the tracking link and send it to whoever is receiving the goods. No account needed.

FAQ

Common Questions

Where do I find my Tracking ID?
It's printed on your shipment receipt when goods are booked. Format: NG-XXXX-LOS-IBD.
Can I share the tracking link?
Yes! On the tracker page, tap "Copy" to get the full URL. Anyone with the link can follow the shipment live.
How long does Lagos to Ibadan take?
Typically 4–6 hours depending on traffic. TrackNG shows live progress so you always know where the goods are.
Are other routes available?
Lagos–Abuja, Lagos–Benin, and Ibadan–Kano are coming soon.
