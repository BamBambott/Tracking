TrackNG • Home • How It Works TRACK SHIPMENT EXPLAINER How TrackNG Works From the moment your goods leave Lagos to the second they arrive in Ibadan — here's exactly what happens. THE PROCESS 01 Goods Are Registered When a shipment is booked, the goods are assigned a unique Tracking ID (e.g. NG-2604-LOS-IBD). This ID is tied to the route, sender, and recipient details. 02 Checkpoints Are Logged As the vehicle moves along the route, agents at each checkpoint scan or confirm the goods. The system records the timestamp and updates the status instantly. 03 You Track in Real-Time Enter your Tracking ID on TrackNG and see exactly which checkpoint your goods have reached. The status updates live — no need to refresh. 04 Share With Your Recipient Copy the tracking link and send it to whoever is receiving the goods. They can follow along in real-time without needing an account. Your Tracking ID is printed on your shipment receipt when the goods are booked. It looks like NG-XXXX-LOS-IBD. Can I share the tracking link? Yes! On the tracker page, use the "Copy Link" button to copy the full URL. Anyone with the link can follow the shipment live. How long does Lagos to Ibadan take? Typically 4–6 hours depending on traffic, especially around Sagamu and the expressway. TrackNG shows live progress so you always know where the goods are. Is this available for other routes? Lagos–Ibadan is the active route. Lagos–Abuja, Lagos–Benin, and Ibadan–Kano are coming soon. Ready to track? Enter your Tracking ID and follow your goods in real-time.
